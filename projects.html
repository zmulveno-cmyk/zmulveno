Phantoms

For this project, we had to recreate a poem from the text in The Plato's Allegory. “The release from the bonds, the turning around from the shadows, to the phantoms and the light from the cave to the sun; the divine appearances, the things that are, rather that as before phantoms cast by a light”. One consistent theme from Plato’s Allegory that stood out to me is the illusion of reality and how one perceives what to be true. "... of the objects which are being carried in like manner, they would only see the shadows... if they were able to converse with one another, would they not suppose that they were naming what was actually before them....". This quote to me emphasizes the fact that the prisoner uses abstract knowledge while being in the state of darkness. This darkness pertains to ignorance so, in this state the prisoners still try to gain some truth of their surroundings. Unknowing to what these shadows are being controlled for them by something greater than what they can imagine. “To them, I said, the truth would be literally nothing but the shadows of the images." Here Socrates explains that the prisoner would have no idea what is beyond the shadows and darkness. All along the shadows was just an extension of the light, making us question more than what our eyes allowed us to see. Making you question how this could be along and you had no idea. This aporic feeling of not knowing anything about anything. Like finding out you aren’t really who you were told you are. A shift in your existence or new learning material for your brain. Depending on how you see things. In my poem the part “the turning over from the shadows” expresses the change from light to dark. The enlightenment of the brain. Knowledge. Seeing what is, for what it is. Like getting to the other side of the mountain. Like seeing both sides of the coin. Yet, sometimes this harsh reality is hard to settle into. Like when the prisoner finally leaves the cage and sees beyond shadows. He saw the sun, grass, flowers, water, rocks and everything for the first time, it was a sense of freedom. A sense of knowing not knowing all at once. Reflecting back to once was and trying to understand the was and now at the same time. Not being bound to another prisoner, day upon day and only knowing nothing but what was. When the sunlight hit his skin, his mind was opened to what casted the shadows in the cave. This darkness is casted by light. “The divine appearance”. The word divine can be interchangeable for a few di erent meanings. 1. Godly or godlike, 2. To discover by intuition; insight. Nowthe divine appearance can be referred to as both. Once the prisoner looks from the light to the shadow this “divine appearance” is apparent. Once discovering this outside force was really in control of what he thought to be true. Unlike the prisoners who were still their in a controlled environment, unaware of what lie right behind them. “The thing that are, rather than as before.” “The things that are” refer to truth, wisdom and ideas etc. “Rather than as before” when darkness was the one truth. The term “Dark Ages” was given this name because of the lack of intellectual and cultural advance. The Renaissance came and with that came enlightenment as well. The “Enlightenment Period” refers to the free thinking and questions of oneself and their surroundings. Just like when the prisoner escaped the cave. Dark Ages to the Enlightenment period with just a walk up the stairs from the cave. The sense of enlightenment and knowledge of its surroundings but also questioning what's around them. “Phantom cast by a light”. Phantoms can be indicated as spirit or soul. Without the light we are unable to see anything. We are unable to see or know from right or wrong. Without seeing or knowing there is no light and dark. Without the balance perhaps there would be nothing at all In myartwork, I wanted to emphasize the dark and light. In the background of the poem, I went for a simple approach. I didn’t want anything to overpower the poem. In the middle of the bottom part, I added a butterfly to symbolize a human soul. In Greek mythology, Psyche, a mortal woman goes through all the tests to see her love Eros again. Psyche's willingness to go trial after trial to see her love again. This shows humans eternal stride to what motivates them. I put the butterfly between both light and dark to mirror the prisoner's journey from light to the dark. Also making the sun that's behind the butterfly kinda bright so it stands out. While in the corner, the left part of the wing is in the darkness.“The only true wisdom is in knowing you know nothing.” The ying and the yang
